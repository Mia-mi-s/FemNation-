Feminism today is more inclusive and connected than ever. It continues to fight for women's rights, but it also focuses on a wider range of issues: Gender equality in workplaces and schools. The right to make decisions about one's own body. Representation in politics, media, and leadership. Equal sharing of family responsibilities. Ending gender-based violence and online harassment. Modern feminism also highlights the importance of intersectionality — the idea that gender equality can't be separated from race, sexuality, disability, or economic background. Social media has become a powerful tool for spreading feminist ideas and creating change. Young activists use platforms like TikTok, Instagram, and Twitter to raise awareness and hold leaders accountable. Feminism today isn't about one voice or one issue — it's a global movement shaped by millions of people who believe equality should be a reality, not just an idea.
