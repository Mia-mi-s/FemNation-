1. What Is Feminism Feminism is the belief that all people — regardless of gender — deserve equal rights, opportunities, and respect. It's about breaking down barriers that prevent people from reaching their potential because of gender expectations. Feminism is not about putting one gender above another. It's about creating balance and fairness in every part of life — from the workplace to politics, education, relationships, and beyond. Feminists work to challenge discrimination, stereotypes, and systems that limit anyone. This includes fighting for women's rights, but also for men, non-binary, and transgender people whose freedoms are affected by gender inequality. At its heart, feminism is about choice, respect, and equality — for everyone.
